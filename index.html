It began with a trip

Nobody planned it. A sudden winter trip to Nawabganj — fog on the fields, cold air, the kind of quiet that only exists in villages in December. I went because I had to. I stayed because of her.

Page 2
The night we didn't sleep

Ludo. Monopoly. Noodles at 2AM she made without being asked. We stayed up all night talking about everything and nothing, the hours disappearing like they were embarrassed to interrupt us.

Page 3
The river, the mud, the flowers

She swam. I watched from the bank, quietly happy just to be there. Then I slipped in the mud getting up. A spectacular, dignified fall. She laughed. Later I picked flowers for her in a field that smelled like winter and hope.

Page 4
She wasn't into me yet

I knew how I felt long before she did. I picked those flowers for a girl who hadn't said yes yet, carried them across a muddy field, and handed them over like it was the most natural thing in the world. Because for me, it was.

Page 5
One whole year

And now here we are. A year of mornings and late nights, of being found when we got separated, of building something that belongs to just us. She is my favourite story. This is only the beginning of it.

🌫️ Winter · Nawabganj · Dhaka Chapter One

How it began

It wasn't planned. The best things never are.


A sudden trip to Nawabganj — cold winter air thick with fog, the fields pale and still, the kind of quiet that only villages get when the season turns. The kind of cold that makes you want to be close to someone. I didn't know yet that this trip would become the story I'd tell for the rest of my life.


She wasn't into me. But somewhere in the mist and the chai and the cold morning light, I was already completely, quietly in love with her. I'm so glad we went.

Chapter Two

The day that changed everything

We never had an official first date. What we had was better — an entire day that just kept going.

Late night
The Graveyard Walk
We walked past a graveyard at night, making jokes like two people who are either very brave or very comfortable with each other. Definitely the second one.
Her home
Ludo, Monopoly & Pure Chaos
Ludo first. Then Monopoly — dhoni howar mojar khela. She bankrupted everyone in the first hour and somehow acted surprised about it. I laughed until it hurt.
2AM kitchen
Noodles & Chaa
She made us noodles and chaa without being asked. Simple, warm, perfect. There's something quietly romantic about someone making food for you at 2AM like it's nothing.
All night
We Didn't Sleep
We stayed up all night. I don't remember every word. I just remember not wanting any of it to stop.
Morning
Separated & Reunited
We moved houses and got separated. Worst few minutes of the whole day. Then we found each other again and everything felt lighter.
The river
The River & The Mud Incident 😭
She swam. I watched from the bank, quietly happy just to be there. Then getting up, I slipped in the wet mud. A full, spectacular, completely dignified fall. Mortifying. Worth it forever.
Flower field
I Picked Flowers for Her
We went to a flower field. I picked some for her and we walked together, laughing at nothing. She wasn't into me yet. I was completely, irrevocably in love — picking flowers like they were the most important things I'd ever held.
